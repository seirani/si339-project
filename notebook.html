I've always felt that my love for art and passion for writing about it originated in the golden memories I have of books and movies and TV shows that I absorbed during childhood. This piece takes a deep dive on those influences, especially in regards to the presence of the mystery genre in the media I read and watch in my ongoing, fruitless attempt to perpetually follow in my sister's footsteps.

I wrote this very lengthy piece on a bit of a spiritual awakening I had at a Lorde concert, and the impact that her music has had on my life through the ebbs and flows of my teenage-dom. Maybe it's because I wrote this right around the summer solstice and because it's about the "Solar Power" tour, but this one just radiates good summer energy for me, and is full of perfectly luminous memories.

This is the first piece I ever wrote for The Michigan Daily, one that will always be very near and dear to my heart. It's one of my most raw and vulnerable pieces of writing, dissecting one of my favorite songs, Phoebe Bridgers' "I Know the End," during a period of time where it felt like not only was my life falling apart, but the rest of the world too.
